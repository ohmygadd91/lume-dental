What Are Porcelain Veneers?

Porcelain veneers are wafer-thin ceramic shells — typically 0.3 to 0.5 millimetres — custom-fabricated to bond permanently over the front surface of your teeth. They correct chips, gaps, discoloration, and minor misalignment in a single restorative arc.

Each veneer at Lume Dental is hand-layered by our partnered ceramist atelier using feldspathic or lithium disilicate porcelain, chosen for its ability to mimic the translucency and light refraction of natural enamel.

Step by Step

The Process

01

Digital Smile Design

Using intraoral scanning and AI-powered modelling, we create a virtual preview of your new smile before any preparation begins.

02

Minimally Invasive Preparation

A conservative 0.3–0.5mm layer of enamel is gently reshaped. Temporary veneers are placed while your custom shells are fabricated.

03

Ceramist Fabrication

Our partner atelier hand-layers each veneer over 7–10 days, building translucency, texture, and characterisation unique to your anatomy.

04

Precision Bonding

Using resin cement under isolation protocol, each veneer is permanently bonded and light-cured. Final adjustments ensure a flawless bite.

Common Questions

How much tooth enamel is removed?

add

Typically 0.3–0.5mm — about the thickness of a contact lens. This is considered minimally invasive and preserves the vast majority of your natural tooth structure.

Do veneers look natural?

add

When crafted by a skilled ceramist, porcelain veneers are virtually indistinguishable from natural teeth. The layered porcelain mimics enamel's translucency and depth.

Can veneers stain?

add

High-quality porcelain is highly resistant to staining — more so than natural enamel. However, the bonding cement at the margins can discolour over time if oral hygiene is neglected.

Are veneers reversible?

add

Because a thin layer of enamel is removed during preparation, traditional veneers are considered irreversible. We also offer no-prep veneers for select cases where no enamel removal is needed.
