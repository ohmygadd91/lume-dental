Restorative Science

What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a small titanium or zirconia post surgically placed into the jawbone, where it fuses with living bone tissue through a process called osseointegration. This creates an anchor as stable as — or stronger than — a natural tooth root.

Once integrated, a custom abutment and porcelain crown are attached, restoring full function and aesthetics. Unlike bridges, implants preserve adjacent teeth and stimulate the jawbone, preventing the bone loss that follows tooth extraction.

Treatment Options

Implant Types

looks_one

Single Tooth Implant

Replaces an individual missing tooth with a titanium post and custom porcelain crown. The adjacent teeth remain untouched and uncompromised.

linear_scale

Implant Bridge

For 3+ consecutive missing teeth, two implant posts support a fixed porcelain bridge — eliminating the need for a removable partial denture.

select_all

Full-Arch Restoration

The All-on-4 or All-on-6 protocol anchors a complete arch of teeth on just 4–6 strategically placed implants. Fixed, permanent, and life-changing.

How It Works

The Process

01

Diagnostic Imaging

CBCT 3D scanning maps your bone density, nerve pathways, and sinus proximity. This data drives surgical planning with sub-millimetre accuracy.

02

Surgical Placement

Under local anaesthesia or conscious sedation, the titanium post is placed into the jawbone. The procedure typically takes 30–60 minutes per implant.

03

Osseointegration

Over 3–6 months, the implant fuses with your bone at a cellular level. During this period, a temporary restoration maintains aesthetics and function.

04

Abutment & Impression

Once integration is confirmed, a precision abutment is attached and digital impressions are taken for your final crown fabrication.

05

Crown Delivery

Your custom porcelain crown — shade-matched and anatomically contoured — is permanently cemented. The result is indistinguishable from a natural tooth.

Common Questions

Most adults with adequate bone density are candidates. Conditions like uncontrolled diabetes or heavy smoking may require additional assessment. We offer bone grafting for patients with insufficient bone volume.

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia or sedation — most patients report less discomfort than a tooth extraction. Post-operative soreness is typically managed with over-the-counter pain relief.

From placement to final crown, the process typically takes 4–6 months to allow for complete osseointegration. Immediate-load protocols may be available for select cases.

Implants require the same care as natural teeth — brushing, flossing, and regular professional cleanings. There is no special maintenance required.
